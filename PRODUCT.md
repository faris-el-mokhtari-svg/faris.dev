# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two arrival states, both skeptical in different ways:

**The burned restaurant owner.** Has already paid an agency, gotten a mediocre website, and is still managing reservations on paper. Arrives doubtful. Needs proof before trust — not promises, not adjectives. They've heard "fast delivery and great results" before. What wins them over is specificity: a real system, described in terms of their actual workflow.

**The local business owner who doesn't know where to start.** Knows they need a digital presence but finds the landscape overwhelming. Not sure what to ask for. Needs the site to make the path feel clear and achievable — not to throw jargon at them. Confidence comes from Deploy clearly knowing what they're doing, not from the owner needing to figure it out first.

Both are reading on their phones, in between shifts or during a quiet moment. German speakers. They are not evaluating design systems; they are deciding whether to trust someone with their business.

## Product Purpose

Deploy builds complete business operating systems for gastronomy and local SMBs — not websites as standalone deliverables.

For a café client, this means: website with self-edit capability, reservation system, admin dashboard, email setup, and Lightspeed POS integration so the owner can track sales and inventory in one place. The website is the visible entry point; the integrated system is the actual product.

Success is when the client makes more revenue without struggling with any technical layer. Deploy owns the complexity so the client never has to.

## Brand Personality

Capable, direct, unfussy. The agency that shows up, builds the thing, and leaves it working. Not interested in impressing other designers — interested in results the client can point to. In three words: **precise, grounded, effective**.

Voice is plain-spoken and confident. No hedging, no jargon, no upsell language. Speaks to a restaurant owner the way a skilled tradesperson would — not the way a consultant would.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate consultancy.** Navy palettes, stock photos of suited people in meeting rooms, trust-signal icon rows (checkmarks, shields, star ratings), language that says "solutions" and "synergy." The aesthetic of a company that charges for decks.
- **AI-slop agency sites.** Hero-metric templates (big stat + gradient accent), identical icon-heading-text card grids, gradient text on the wordmark, glassmorphism as decoration, the word "innovative" used without evidence.
- **Boring local web shops.** White backgrounds, small fonts, generic testimonial carousels, no design conviction. Indistinguishable from the 40 other agencies in the same city.

## Design Principles

**Show the system, not the deliverable.** The instinct is to list services (website, SEO, support). The right move is to describe the operating system — reservation + inventory + dashboard + self-edit — in the client's language. One integrated thing, not a menu of parts.

**Earn skepticism before asking for trust.** The burned client doesn't respond to promises. They respond to specificity. Every claim needs a mechanism behind it: not "fast delivery" but "live in 14 days"; not "we handle everything" but here's what the café owner sees in their dashboard on Tuesday morning.

**Complexity is Deploy's job, not the client's.** The interface and the copy should both radiate this. Nothing on the site should require the user to understand how something works technically. They should only need to understand what it does for their business.

**Revenue is the only metric that matters.** Features are means; more customers, higher average spend, fewer missed reservations are the end. Design communicates outcomes, not specs.

**Practice what you preach.** Deploy builds operating systems for clients. The Deploy site should itself feel like a complete, considered system — not a brochure. Every page should reflect the same quality of thought the client is being promised.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA baseline. Primary audience is German-speaking; `lang="de"` is set on the root element. Large body type (deliberately chosen) improves readability for non-technical users reading quickly on mobile. No known specific accommodation requirements beyond standard AA compliance.
